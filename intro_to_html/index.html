Information and Resources for New Voters

With the 2024 election, a new generation of voters are coming to the stage. Now, people born from the years 2003 to 2006 are able to register and participate in the United States general election. Here, you can find resources and answers to questions to help you navigate this exciting new privilege.

Why Should I Vote?

As an American citizen, you have many civil duties that our government asks or requires of you. These range from obeying federal, state, and local laws, paying taxes, and even driving safely. Participating in the democratic process is one of thee duties, and is one of the most important. By voting you are able to share your voice and choose who you want to represent your voice in a government office. There are many things you can vote for, from county sherrifs to mayors to governors and to, of course, president. Voting is extremely important, especially if you aspire to seeing certain changes made in your communities and broader country.

What the Requirements to Vote?

  • You must be a U.S. Citizen. Citizens residing in U.S. territories, however, cannot vote in the Presidential Election.
  • You must meet state residency requirements
  • You Must be 18 years old by Election Day. This year it is Tuesday, November 5th, 2024.
  • You must not have convicted a felony, though specific rules vary in each state
  • You must not be actively serving a sentence for a previously committed crime. However, this also varies state to state.

How Do I Register to Vote?

The easiest way to register to vote is online. Every state has an online voter registration system you can go to to register. These sites URL's will end with .gov, so make sure you are on the right site.

Where Do I Go To Vote?

Where you go to vote on Election Day depends on your specific address and county. Each zip code as a dedicated polling location that the residents of that zip code go to vote.

How Do I Know Who To Vote For?

There are many ways to find information on the presidential candidates. Unless you follow the election very closely throughout the entire process, you only need to spend time researching the two primary candidates for our country's two main political parties. These parties are the Democratic and Republican parties. You might also see them called the blue and red parties respectively and the members referred to as Democrats and Republicans. The Republican Party is also often called the GOP. In terms of mascots, the Democratic Party is represented by a donkey, and the Republican Party is represented by an elephant.

As of August 2024, the candidates for this years election are current Vice President Kamala Harris and Former President Donald Trump. Minnesota Governor, Tim Walz, and Senator J.D. Vance are their respective running mates.

The easiest way to make your decision is by researching the two primary candidates. Policies and backgrounds are especially important, as they may reflect your personal values and beliefs. It is tradition for the candidates to have 2 scheduled debates hosted by different news sources, so watching those is an excellent way to see and hear the candidates talk about their ideas and policies. You might also want to tune into the Democratic and Republican National Conventions. There, the candidates and significant party members will give speeches also detailing that party's values.

The first debate scheduled for the current presidential candidates is September 10th, 2024.

Of course, the best way is for you to do your own personal resarch outside of news sources and media outlets. There are many sources that will provide unbiased facts and information about every candidate.
